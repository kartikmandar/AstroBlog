Medieval Finitism

"As far as I remember Aristotle, you promised to discuss the possibility of our universe being finite? ", inquires T.

"Nah, T; I already explained in the book about the infinitism of the universe.", asserted Aristotle.


  • The medieval primordial infinitism model(i.e. our universe is infinitely large) remained prevalent for a long time. Such was the belief among the Greeks upon Aristotle that they refused to accept any other model for a very long time.



    • Treatise De Luce(On Light)

    T:- Hi Robert. I heard a lot about your treatise De Luce. Do shine a light on it?

    Robert:- Thanks T. Basically what I wanted to convey is that the universe doesn't need to have infinite age! We can assume that the universe came into being from an explosion. Then it could be further inferred that all the stars and planets formed when the matter crystallizes(cooled down) in a set of nested spheres around Earth.


  • Robert Grosseteste was the first person to attempt to describe the heavens using a single set of physical laws.

    • Then for nearly 4 centuries, the realm of cosmology was in eerie silence without any fresh discoveries.



    Olbers Paradox

    Give this picture a quick look. Wait before you stare at the photo, do you know what a line of sight is?


    Take a straight long line emerging from your eyes( say for strictly experimental purposes; you can assume yourself to be akin to superman). Now wherever that line(or laser) falls, that object is said to be in your line of sight(dunno why the screen hasn't yet melted!).


    Back to the picture; wherever you could see, your line of sight will eventually meet a tree's trunk.


  • To all the people, who love to nitpick, saying that they could see a pixel or two which doesn't look like trees(probably the sky or mountain behind); I would love to argue with them in the comments!


    • But everyone can agree with me on one thing:- the sky is dark at night. Right? Trivial but this very fact was used by Johannes Kepler in 1610 to conclude a striking observation.


    T:- Johannes; isn't it so serene? The night sky brimming with stars on a new moon. I am in complete awe!

    Kepler:- True T. What amazes me is that this infinite universe has unlimited stars and yet we can only behold the beauty of a few thousand! Wait, T, if there are infinite stars why can't we see all of them?

    T:- Probably they are too far away.

    Kepler:- Yes they are indeed far. But as we assume our universe to be static, infinite, and homogeneous; the distance would not matter as far as brightness is concerned.

    T:- Hmm...I doubt if this fact is valid. I would barely see a candle from a mile away but I can very well notice it if it's right next to me.

    Kepler:- :) Haha.. let me explain...Let's consider concentric spherical shells(i.e. one beach ball inside another & so on). For a second assume it to be our boundless universe.


    The surface area of each concentric shell (balls) will increase four times if the radius is doubled. Assuming each outer shell to be twice the inner one and the universe to be homogeneous, we could conclude that the outer shell will have 4 times the number of stars. But as these stars are twice as far away they would have 1/4th the brightness.

    One can see the net effect neatly comes out to be the same.


    1star=1nits (only for explanation purposes)


    Inner Shell

    No. of stars = 1

    Distance = same = r

    Brightness = No. of stars * Reciprocal of distance squared = 1 * 1 / r² = 1 / r²

    Outer Shell

    No. of stars = 4

    Distance = twice = 2r

    Brightness = No. of stars* Reciprocal of distance squared = 4 * 1 / 4r² = 1 / r²


    We can see their brightness turns out to be equal. This means that all the shells(balls) will portray the same overall brightness.

    Now as the universe is infinite it must have infinite shells and stars.

    Thus no matter where we look our line of sight must meet at the surface of a star(even though it can be very far).

    And as we just showed the overall brightness of the shell will always remain the same.


    So shouldn't the whole sky just light up like day?

  • This idea didn't become widespread until the 19th century when Heinrich Olbers developed it further and this came to be knowns as Olbers Paradox.
  • The modern explanation of this paradox is that some stars are so far away from us that the light they emit has not still reached us. So rather than proving the universe is finite in space the paradox concluded it must be finite in age!


    • Milky Way- A lone wolf?

    T:- Beautiful isn't it Edwin, the M31, such a magnificent nebula (cloud of gas and dust)!

    Hubble:- You know T, it's not a nebula, it's a galaxy just like our Milky Way and is called Andromeda galaxy. It is twice as big as ours.

    T:- Yes, yes I read your article in 1923. But how does it concern us; galaxy or nebulae, it's still outta our reach!

    Hubble:- To be honest it does matter, go back 5 years and nobody would believe in the existence of outer space. It's really important to know that the cosmos is much much more than just our barred spiral. For our theories of cosmos to explain the observations satisfactorily we want a homogeneous universe.

    Let's count the number of galaxies above a minimum brightness in some defined area of the sky. This number that we counted should turn out to be the same for other parts of the sky with the same area!

    T:- That's cool!

  • This fact is indeed true but only holds for galaxies that are in our local neighbourhood, for much farther objects the observations deviate from this rule.


    • Hubble's Law

    "You must have heard the siren of an ambulance? If I ask you to close your eyes and tell whether it's coming towards you or moving away, will you be able to answer correctly?

    For most, the answer would be an easy yes. How is it that we are so inherently adapted to distinguish the nuances of sounds? We should thank our ability to the positioning of our ears and the brain's ability to analyze the phase difference in sounds coming from each ear.

    But this is not what concerns us.

    The very fact that we can distinguish is because of the Doppler Effect.

    Take up a rubber band and fix one of its ends. Now move the rubber band towards or away from yourself(don't break it). It gets stretched!

    Similarly, the sound waves also do get stretched however the effect is different for different directions, unlike rubber bands!

    So we perceive those sounds differently. The frequency( shrillness) increases if the sound emitter is coming toward us while it decreases in the latter case.


    You can just ditto this fact in the case of light waves. Here rather than saying frequency increases or decreases we use the terms blue and redshift respectively.

    Galaxies moving towards us are blue-shifted.

    While those moving away are red-shifted. "


    *clapping noises*

    T:- Wonderful Edwin but what's the significance of this?

    Hubble:- T, you sure jump to conclusions prematurely! :)

    In 1920 I found out that the amount of redshift in galaxies was dependent on their distance from us. If their redshift is from the Doppler effect then this suggests that galaxies move away from each other at speeds proportional to their distance, i.e. the universe is expanding as a whole!

    Now let's reverse engineer the process. If the universe is expanding, shouldn't it be smaller than in the past! Then if we extrapolate enough there must exist a time when all of the observable universe must be so small that it all should have been a point.

    Recent calculations do support this belief.

    Ignoring the mathematics behind we could say if H is Hubble's law constant then 1/H or H^-1 would be approximately the age of the universe.



    Background Radiation

    "Voila, voila T it's there", Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson exclaimed together.

    T:- What's there?

    Both:- The universal microwave background radiation; George Gamow was right!

  • You would have felt the shockwave of big fireworks or even a big tire bursting near you!

  • In 1940 George Gamow told if the universe came in with a bang; there must be a shockwave present! The cosmic microwave background radiation is that same shockwave discovered by Robert and Arno in 1979.

  • To be honest, this is not exactly true, the radiation was a byproduct of inflation but as all of this text is oversimplified pardon me for the partial truth!



    • Isotropy of Universe

    Apart from cosmic microwave background radiation, the even distribution of radio waves, X-rays, faint galaxies, and Hubble's Law all point to the fact that our universe is isotropic(similar properties everywhere) and homogeneous.



    Age of Universe

    The age of the universe calculated from Hubble's Law comes out to be between 10-15 billion years. Quite surprisingly the oldest stars and clusters in our galaxy are also 10-15 billion years old.

    This is strong evidence that Hubble's law is due to expansion and the oldest stars formed in the very early days of the universe.



    Helium Abundance

    Some theories also provide an acceptable amount of abundance of elements. Observation shows the oldest objects contain about 25% Helium(the rest is mostly hydrogen). And the computations from theories yield Helium abundance of the same quantity.



    Cosmological Principle

    There is an unspoken confirmation among astronomers. Although a far-reaching assumption, they believe in the cosmological principle. It states that the properties and behavior of objects become regular after we zoom out a lot,i.e., looking at the universe as a whole.

    Though the cosmological principle does put useful constraints on the large variety of possible cosmological theories it doesn't come into play if we are considering less than a few thousand galaxies!

    This principle is closely related to homogeneity and isotropy of the universe but not exactly synonymous. Like the Copernican principle, it concludes that our position in the universe is in no way special.

    Big bang(tbh it feels that they purposely used the most generic name possible)

    At the earliest times, the temperatures and pressure were so immense that all the theories to explain the process just fails.


    The universe at that time was hot and dense but peaceful. There was nothing to disturb it! Neither time nor space was created just then.

    It was like a child in a mother's womb, full of life but peaceful.


    Then something happened, something quite extraordinary that we can't comprehend or explain to date. The peaceful universe lost its equilibrium. This was the very birth of time and space as we know it.

    Who did that, and how did it happen? No one knows. Some say it's God, others claim we are just some experiment. I will say we don't know just yet.


    If the universe would have been perfectly balanced( as all things should be ~T) we would not have been here. There was some defect in this symmetry.


    Believe me, if I say all matter has its antimatter counterpart and if they meet, they annihilate each other, releasing energy (equivalent to their masses E=mc^2) in the process.


    For some reason (defective symmetry) the matter particles were more than the anti-matter particles by 1.000000001%. If they would have been the same in number, they would have quickly annihilated themselves, and that my dear friend would have been the end of the story.

    However, that was not the case; as a result, 0.000000001% of matter particles lived on to become the brick and cement of all the objects in our universe that exists now.

    This difference in symmetry occurred 0.00000000000000000000000000000000001 s after the birth. This led to a process known as inflation.

    Due to the consequence of breaking fundamental symmetries, the dominant force became the zero-point energy( basically the vibrational energy the atoms still possess even at 0°K) of a quantum field.

    You know the speed of light, it's 299792458 m/s. The speed of light abbreviated as c is fundamentally the fastest thing possible. But inflation was faster and still didn't violate Einstein's relativity!

    Because when inflation occurred the universe itself expanded, creating time and space in the process. This is not speed in the traditional sense. The context of measuring speed doesn't exist here as the very physical laws that we use were being generated as the universe expanded.

    In the small-time the inflation continued, the universe doubled in size about 80 times; almost an increase of the factor of 10^78.


    You remember the non-symmetries that made the zero-point energy dominant! This was the very reason for the occurrence of inflation, but as the universe expanded those differences in symmetries diluted to an almost non- perceivable extent, as a result, we see quite a homogeneous universe now.

    As it expanded, albeit, at a much slower rate when inflation ended, it slowly cooled down enough that the known physical principles can be applied.

    During the initial days, the universe was a cascading soup of myriad particles like photons, particle-antiparticle pairs colliding, annihilating, and transforming into one another.


    *The threshold temperature is the temperature below which particles of a given type are no longer produced.


    Hadron era:- It lasted from 10^-8 to 10^-4 s and most of the building blocks of atomic nuclei, protons, and neutrons formed in this era.


    Threshold temperature: T=10^12K(reached t=10^-4s) Hardons include protons, neutrons, and mesons.


    Lepton era:- It lasted from 10^-4 s to 1s. Photon energies became large enough to form light particles such as electron-positron pairs.

    Due to symmetry breakdown, some electrons left over-produced into the present astronomical bodies. Neutrino decoupling (stopped interacting) took place.


    Radiation era:- Lasted from 1s to 1 million years(temperature varies from 10^10K to 40,000°K. Electromagnetic radiation started. Within a few hundred seconds helium was produced.


    Radiation decoupling( Matter era):- 1 million years to the present date

    Stars, galaxies, planets, and human life formed. The dark age started after the temperature reached a few thousand degrees and remained till the first stars started igniting.
